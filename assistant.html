Popular Questions

The Wachau Valley is home to many excellent wineries. Some of the most renowned include Domäne Wachau, FX Pichler, Rudi Pichler, and Weingut Knoll. Most offer tastings and tours, and we can arrange these as part of your vacation package.

The Danube Cycle Path (Donauradweg) is mostly flat and suitable for all fitness levels. There are also more challenging routes through the vineyards and hills for experienced cyclists. We can recommend routes based on your experience and preferences.

The best time to visit the Wachau region is from late spring (May) to early autumn (October). Summer (June-August) offers warm weather perfect for biking and outdoor activities, while autumn (September-October) is ideal for wine enthusiasts as it's harvest season.

Our vacation packages typically include accommodation, breakfast, selected activities (like wine tastings or guided tours), bike rentals if requested, restaurant reservations, and a comprehensive vacation plan document. We handle all the bookings for you, and you pay one total amount that includes our service fee.

Must-see attractions include Dürnstein Castle ruins, Melk Abbey, Göttweig Abbey, the historic towns of Krems and Spitz, and the terraced vineyards of the Wachau Valley. Don't miss a river cruise on the Danube to see the valley from a different perspective.

We recommend at least 3-5 days to fully experience the Wachau region. This allows time for wine tasting, biking along the Danube, visiting historic sites, and enjoying the local cuisine without feeling rushed.
