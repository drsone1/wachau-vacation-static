About the Wachau Region

The Wachau is a picturesque valley in Lower Austria, formed by the Danube river. This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for its stunning landscapes, historic towns, and world-class vineyards.

The region encompasses the Wachau valley itself, along with the nearby Krems and Kamptal areas, each offering unique experiences for visitors:

  • Wachau Valley: Famous for its terraced vineyards, apricot orchards, and medieval castles
  • Krems: A charming city with a historic center, contemporary art, and excellent restaurants
  • Kamptal: Known for its exceptional wines and beautiful countryside perfect for hiking
